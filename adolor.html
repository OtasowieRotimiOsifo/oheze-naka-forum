Oheze Naka: Education

Adolo primary school Oheze naka, Edo state of Nigeria is a state run school.
It was built in 1954 by the Indigenes and handed over toBenin Divisional Council
of old Western Region in 1955. It consists of 2 blocks of 6 classrooms which are
now in complete state of disrepair as seeing in the pictures below.

As of 2016, the school had enrolment of 206 pupils and 8 teachers but a recent
visit to the village speak disaster, only 26 pupils are in the school. The reason
for this was the calamity that befell the school in 2016 when rainstorm destroyed a
block of three classes. There were appeals to the government with no response.In 2017,
window from the almost destroyed classroom fell on two pupils and break their hands
and feet, hence the withdrawal of pupils from the school to safe ones.
